10th & 12th April 2020 – Good Friday and Easter Sunday
LATEST UPDATE FROM REV SUE
Well here we are at the Easter weekend, and still separated, but hopefully together in our Lord.
This weekend though is something to celebrate and we will be doing it by launching ‘CBT’ – Church By Telephone! Having run a trial last weekend, which want well, I will be doing two services via the telephone.
One on Good Friday at 2.30pm, which will be a meditation for Good Friday, if you want to join in saying the Psalm please have this News Sheet with you – Psalm 22.1-11 (see page 2). It would be good to have a cross with you to focus on.
On Easter Sunday at 10.30am there will be a service of prayer and praise. We will use the readings in this News Sheet, and the service sheet that you will also have received. It is simple to join in, but it is up to you if you do so or not, no one will know. For this service I suggest you have a candle to light if you are able. Here are the instructions for joining the services.
Instructions
You will be joining a virtual ‘conference room’ facilitated by WhyPay. You will be able to hear the service, but anything you say will not be heard by anyone on the telephone, only those physically with you. So if you want to put your phone on ‘speaker phone’ so you can put it down, so that those with you can listen that is fine, and no one else will hear you talking to the dog, or your clock chiming etc.
To start please telephone 0333 0110 945 – you will be asked for the Conference Room number, which
is: 78397412# - the # key is most important (found on the bottom right of most phone pads), - you will then be asked for a Pin, which
is: 1376# - again don’t forget the # key.
These steps ensure the security of the ‘room’ and that only those invited can be 2 w15 part of the ‘conference’ or in our case, service.

Once you are in you just listen. If you happen to be the first one to dial in, you will be told you are on your own but please wait for others. If you dial in before the service starts you may just hear silence, please be patient, I will start at the appointed time (by my clock ☺).
You can join even after the service has started and leave (by putting the phone down/disconnecting the call) at any time., and no one will know!
Just in case you were wondering (and I’m sure some of you will!) music is not an option. I have tried it and it sounds as though it is being played under water! So, sorry - you will have to provide your own.
This will become the pattern for future Sundays with a service each week at 10.30am for anyone who wants to be part of it.

PSALM FOR GOOD FRIDAY – 22.1-11

1 My God, my God, why have you forsaken me,
• and are so far from my salvation, from the words of my distress?
2 O my God, I cry in the daytime, but you do not answer;
• and by night also, but I find no rest.
3 Yet you are the Holy One,
• enthroned upon the praises of Israel.
4 Our forebears trusted in you;
• they trusted, and you delivered them.
5 They cried out to you and were delivered;
• they put their trust in you and were not confounded.
6 But as for me, I am a worm and no man,
• scorned by all and despised by the people.
7 All who see me laugh me to scorn;
• they curl their lips and wag their heads, saying,
8 ‘He trusted in the Lord; let him deliver him;
• let him deliver him, if he delights in him.’
9 But it is you that took me out of the womb
• and laid me safe upon my mother’s breast.
10 On you was I cast ever since I was born;
• you are my God even from my mother’s womb.
11 Be not far from me, for trouble is near at hand
• and there is none to help.
Glory to the Father and to the Son and to the Holy Spirit.
As it was in the beginning is now and shall be for ever
Amen.

But go to my brothers and say to them, “I am ascending to my Father and your Father, to my God and your God.“’ 18Mary Magdalene went and announced to the disciples, ‘I have seen the Lord’; and she told them that he had said these things to her.

PRAYERS FROM THE DEAN OF OUR CATHEDRAL - The Very Reverend Andrew Nunn
A prayer in lockdown
The doors of the house where the disciples had met were locked. (John 20.19)
Ever present God,
be with us in our isolation,
be close to us in our distancing,
be healing in our sickness,
be joy in our sadness,
be light in our darkness,
be wisdom in our confusion,
be all that is familiar when all is unfamiliar,
that when the doors reopen
we may with the zeal of Pentecost
inhabit our communities
and speak of your goodness
to an emerging world.
For Jesus’ sake. Amen.

Please pray for all who are involved in the response to this crisis worldwide.
Loving God,
source of healing and comfort,
fill us with your grace,
that the sick may be made whole,
that those who care for us may be strengthened,
that the anxious may be calmed,
and those most vulnerable be protected
in the power of Spirit
in the faith of Jesus Christ our Lord. Amen.
